About.

Hi there! I'm Hope. As a studying data scientist with background in applied mathematics and computer science, my goal is to promote social good through applications in the medical field, non-profits, space exploration, cybersecurity, and ethically designing and applying algorithms.

Interests.

  • Statistical epidemiology and related system dynamics
  • Astrostatistics
  • Algorithm design, ethical machine learning and artificial intelligence
  • Honest, accessible, digestible, and impactful visualization
  • Bayesian methodologies and inference
  • Neural Networks and Ensemble Methods
  • Data privacy, usage, and cybersecurity
  • Criminal forensics
I am currently studying at Harvard University for my Master's of Science in Data Science. I hold a Bachelor of Science from Marist College in Data Science & Analytics and Applied Mathematics.
I've done short academic research for courses on ecological bayesian analysis, dynamic system modeling with epidemiology (COVID), sentiment analysis as applied to news truthfulness, cosmological machine learning, and an expose on Twin Primes. I have also officially researched unpaid through PolyMath Jr., working with Markov Chains, artificial intelligence methods, and abstract algebra to produce a mathematically strategic paper on abstract strategy games Tapatan and Picaria.
